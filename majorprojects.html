Rethinking Penile Implants

Erectile dysfunction (ED) affects 150 million men worldwide, and half of men over the age of 65 age suffer from ED. The implantation of a penile prosthesis can be an effective solution when other treatments such as drug therapy fail or are inappropriate due to health complications or interfering medications.

Current penile implant solutions are outdated and have not seen significant innovation since the 1970's. I worked with Coloplast and a team of 3 interdisciplinary Thayer engineers to rethink penile implants.

  • SKILLS: Research in scientific literature, Market research (manufacturers, surgeons, patients, salespeople), Brainstorming, Testbench development, SolidWorks development, Testing and Refining
  • OUTCOMES: Works-like model and SolidWorks Looks-like model, Brieanna S. Weinstein Engineering Design Prize, selection by the Journal of Sexual Medicine to present as the lead author at the Sexual Medicine Society of North America's (SMSNA) 19th Annual Meetup
